7\. Post documentation showing audio from your Pico

- I do not have images of the current setup yet, but I have started planning the physical components and how the instrument will work with the Raspberry Pi Pico. The main idea is to create a small electronic instrument that reacts to movement and touch.

- One of the parts I plan to use is a recycled toy karaoke microphone. I want to repurpose it as the main handheld part of the instrument so that it feels more like performing with a real microphone rather than just pressing buttons on a board. The microphone casing will mainly act as the physical interface while the actual sound will be generated through the Pico.

8\. Post documentation showing audio from your Pico

- For sound output, I am using two piezo buzzers connected to the Pico. These will be programmed to produce different tones and rhythms using PWM. The idea is that the Pico will control pitch and timing through code so that the buzzers can create simple melodies or beats.

- I am also planning to include a disc or shaker-like component that produces sound when it moves. When the disc shakes, the vibrations will be detected by a sensor and translated into signals that trigger sound from the buzzers. This will allow the instrument to respond to motion, making it feel more interactive and percussive.

9\. Post documentation of your progress on the Musical Instrument

- For the instrumental portion, my main focus has been thinking about how the interaction and sound generation will work together. The Raspberry Pi Pico will control the timing and pitch of the sounds produced by the piezo buzzers. By adjusting the frequency in the code, I can create different musical notes or rhythmic patterns.

- The two piezo buzzers will allow me to experiment with layering sounds. For example, one buzzer could play a melody while the other creates percussion-like sounds or background tones. This should make the instrument sound fuller than using just one buzzer.

10\. Post documentation of your progress on the Musical Instrument

- Another part of the design is incorporating the recycled karaoke microphone to make the instrument more performative and easier to hold. Instead of the instrument being fixed on a table, it can be held and moved, which works well with the shaking disc idea.

- The goal is to combine movement, simple sensors, and programmed sound to create an instrument that responds physically when someone interacts with it.
